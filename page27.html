Innovation Hubs & Labs Meet-Ups: FAQs

What is the purpose of the meet-ups?

The GC innovation hubs and labs community is broad and diverse with a vast range of needs, skills and experiences. With that said, there seems to be two main consistent needs expressed by the group: the need to connect and the need to learn from others.

With that in mind, we propose that the purpose of the meet-ups are to (1) connect with others and (2) share information (lessons learned, challenges, new practices, resources).

Who are the participants of the community?

Over 200 people representing a vast range of levels, skills, experiences and over 20 departments are on the distribution list. Although the meet-ups are targeted for those working in innovation hubs and labs, we are not exclusive and welcome anyone to the meet-ups who demonstrate an interest in being part of this community.

As there is no ‘official’ definition of a GC innovation hubs and labs, participants represent a wide range of teams including digital transformation, Free Agents and GC Entrepreneurs, amongst others.

In order to broaden our skill set, we will also invite those from outside of the GC with experience in innovation hubs and labs to share their perspectives with us and help us grow as innovators in the public service.

Participation at the meet-ups varies: people jump in and out into the meet-ups as needed.

How might we connect and share information?

CSPS will continue to play the role of the “secretariat”, noting that we are an informal group with no terms of reference.

CSPS will support the building of the community and sharing information through a range of means:

● Bi-monthly meet-ups

● GCcollab page

● On Twitter: #GCHubsLabs, #psilabs

● Slack channel: #innovation-in-the-gc-linnovation-au-sein-du-gc

● Additional events  

Interested in presenting at a meet-up?

Our bi-monthly meet-up are the cornerstone of the community. These meet-ups are facilitated sessions designed to create a safe space for sharing, learning and connecting.

We are always looking for presenters to share their experiences, ignite learning and meaningful discussion. Given the audience, the meet-ups also offer an ideal test bed for trying new ways of facilitating, or new tools.

As the meet-ups are designed with a bias towards action, we are happy to work with you to help create this space.
