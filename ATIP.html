Access to Information Compliance

Welcome to the ATIP Compliance Project

Why ATIP?

There is increasing demand for ATI, build up of unknown backlogs and limitation of resources. An ATI compliance analysis engine could automatically compare and associate exemptions to requested information.

Examined a sample of stored ATI data to learn and explore requirements for automation.

Exploration and Findings


We have examined a sample of ATI data stored in Access Pro, most commonly used by departments, to learn and explore requirements for automation.

The following challenges were noted:

 No support for systematic access to documents, pre/post redaction. Manual workaround is only available in a specific software version.

 Original digital representations of documents are inaccessible after system entry, so redactions overlay an image of a document.

 An Optical Character Recognition (OCR)* is a key requirement to be able to depict stored documentation and represents an important progression over the status quo. However, the inability to identify data structure and meaning will likely remain.

Next Steps

Limited use of the data generated in existing ATI management solutions prevents the development of an ATI compliance analysis engine.

Demonstration projects aim to explore opportunities to address horizontal challenges and to pursue scalable solutions. This assessment has revealed a significant limitation in the pursuit of this project as it is scoped, or of any ATI project requiring the retrieval of machine-readable ATI data.
